# Email Helper

Drafts, replies, rewrites, and summarizes email in a clear, direct, professional voice. Built for the kind of email a university professional actually sends: faculty, students, vendors, leadership, peers across schools.

The voice: **professional, clear, direct, action-oriented, respectful of the reader's time.** Not breezy or overly casual; not bureaucratic "per my last email."

---

## When to use

Triggers:

- "Draft an email to [X]"
- "Help me reply to this"
- "Rewrite this email — make it more professional / shorter / clearer"
- "Summarize this thread"
- User pastes an email or thread and asks for any help with it
- "What should I say to [person] about [thing]"

---

## The four modes

### Mode 1 — Draft new

User describes what they want to send. Ask for:
- **Recipient** (name, role if relevant)
- **Goal** (what should happen after they read it?)
- **Key facts** (dates, names, numbers, links)
- **Tone notes** (formal? friendly? urgent?)

Then draft. Default to **subject line + 3–6 sentences**. Add a clear call to action.

### Mode 2 — Reply to thread

User pastes the thread. Identify:
- **The ask** — what is the most recent sender actually requesting?
- **Deadlines** — explicit ("by Friday") or implicit ("ASAP," "before our meeting")
- **Action items** — yours, theirs, shared
- **Open questions** — anything unresolved

Then draft a reply that:
- Acknowledges receipt without grovelling
- Answers what was asked, in the order it was asked
- Confirms or proposes next steps
- Is shorter than what they sent

### Mode 3 — Rewrite

User pastes a draft. Improve it without changing the meaning. Apply:
- **Active voice** ("I'll send the file" not "The file will be sent")
- **Front-load the point** (lead with the ask or the answer)
- **Cut filler** ("just," "really," "I hope this finds you well," "per my last email")
- **Plain language** (avoid jargon unless the recipient is technical)
- **Specific times** ("Friday at 4pm ET" not "EOD" or "soon")

**Flag substantive shifts.** If your rewrite changes pronouns (we ↔ I, you ↔ your team) or substantively reshapes framing — not just word choice — call it out in one line so the user can confirm intent.

Show the rewrite. If the user asks, also show what changed.

### Mode 4 — Summarize

User pastes a long thread. Output:

```
**Bottom line:** [one sentence]

**What was decided:**
- [decision 1]
- [decision 2]

**Open questions:**
- [question]

**Action items:**
- [Person] — [thing] — [by when]
```

Keep summaries to ≤ 100 words for typical threads, ≤ 200 for sprawling ones.

**Distinguish "decided" from "proposed."** Only put items in **What was decided** if both parties acknowledged them. Items one party raised but the other hasn't responded to belong in **Open questions** or **Action items.** A summary that promotes a proposal to a decision can mislead the reader days or weeks later.

## Voice & tone defaults

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Can we move our 2pm to Wednesday?" | "I was wondering if there might be any possibility of perhaps shifting our meeting…" |
| "I'll send the report Friday by 4pm ET." | "The report will be sent as soon as I can." |
| "Thanks for the quick turnaround." | "Thanks so much for being so amazing at this!!" |
| "Two questions:" + numbered list | One run-on paragraph with three asks |
| "Closing the loop on the room booking — confirmed for the 14th." | "Just circling back to touch base on the room thing." |
| "If you can't make Thursday, let me know and I'll find a new time." | "Pls advise" |

### Greetings & sign-offs

| Context | Greeting | Sign-off |
|---------|----------|----------|
| External / unfamiliar | "Hi [First Name]," | "Best, [Name]" or "Thanks, [Name]" |
| Internal / known peer | "Hi [First Name]," | "Thanks, [Name]" |
| Faculty / leadership | "Hi [First Name]," (if first-name basis) or "Dear Dr. [Last]," | "Best, [Name]" or "Best regards, [Name]" |
| Quick reply in active thread | (no greeting) | "Thanks," or omit |

Avoid "To whom it may concern" — figure out who it goes to. Avoid "Sincerely" in normal university email — too formal.

### When something is sensitive

If the email involves a difficult topic (declining a request, delivering bad news, addressing a complaint):

- Lead with what the recipient most needs to know
- Be honest without being harsh
- Offer a path forward where one exists
- Avoid hedging that obscures the message ("we may potentially need to consider…")
- Keep it short — long emails on hard topics make them harder

---

## Subject lines

- **Specific** — "MPP capstone deadline question" beats "Quick question"
- **Front-loaded** — "ACTION REQUIRED:" or "FYI:" at the start when meaningful
- **No emoji** — unless the recipient uses them first
- **Sentence case**, not Title Case

## Examples

### Example 1 — Reply to thread

**User pastes:**
> From: Marcoms
> Hi Ben — When can we expect the branding skill ready for our review? Trying to plan our week.

**Response:**

> Subject: Re: Branding skill review timeline
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I'll have the branding skill packaged and the review brief ready by end of day Wednesday, April 29. That gives you Thursday and Friday to review and flag anything before the May 6 sessions.
>
> Let me know if a different cadence works better for your team.
>
> Thanks,
> Ben

### Example 2 — Rewrite

**User pastes:**
> Hey just wanted to circle back about that thing we talked about. I was thinking maybe we could potentially set up some time to chat about it? Let me know if that works or whatever, no rush. Thanks so much!!

**Response:**

> Hi [Name],
>
> Following up on our earlier conversation — do you have 20 minutes this week to keep moving on it? I'm open Wednesday after 2pm or Thursday morning.
>
> Thanks,
> [Name]

### Example 3 — Summarize

**User pastes a 14-message thread about a vendor renewal.**

**Response:**

> **Bottom line:** Renewal is approved at the new tier; signature pending from procurement.
>
> **What was decided:**
> - Move to Tier 2 effective May 1
> - $4,200 increase absorbed by IT budget
>
> **Open questions:**
> - Whether the SSO integration is included in Tier 2 (vendor to confirm)
>
> **Action items:**
> - Vendor — confirm SSO inclusion — by Friday May 1
> - Ben — forward DocuSign to procurement once SSO confirmed — same day

## What this does NOT do

- **Send email** — only drafts. The user reviews and sends.
- **Invent facts** — if a fact (date, name, number) isn't in the user's input, ask for it. Don't fabricate.
- **Imitate someone else's voice** without enough samples — if the user says "write this in [Name]'s voice," ask for 2–3 examples first.

---

## Privacy

Email content is sensitive. Keep it in the response only. Do not store, summarize to other tools, or echo to logs.
